## 1. Domain Glossary (EN/AR)

### Core Brand & Product Terms

| Term (EN) | Term (AR) | Definition | Usage Notes |
|-----------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| **Roaya IT** | **رؤيا لتقنية المعلومات** | WorldPosta Systems - IT services division focused on infrastructure, cloud, cybersecurity | Always use full name on first mention |
| **WorldPosta** | **وورلد بوستا** | Parent company brand, global email hosting and infrastructure provider | Keep English brand name in Arabic |
| **One SLA** | **اتفاقية خدمة واحدة** | Single Service Level Agreement covering all IT services | Emphasize simplification benefit |
| **CloudEdge Portal** | **بوابة كلاود إيدج** | Multi-tenant cloud management platform | Keep English brand name in Arabic text |
| **Posta Portal** | **بوابة بوستا** | White-label Microsoft Exchange email hosting platform | Keep English brand name in Arabic text |
| **SOC Services** | **خدمات مركز العمليات الأمنية** | Security Operations Center - 24/7 monitoring | Spell out on first mention in Arabic |
| **White-label** | **قابل للتخصيص بعلامتك التجارية** | Rebranding under customer's own brand | Arabic uses descriptive phrase |
| **Multi-tenant** | **متعدد المستخدمين** | Architecture allowing multiple customers to share infrastructure | Literal translation acceptable |
| **Zero-downtime** | **دون انقطاع الخدمة** | Migration ensuring continuous service availability | Emphasize business continuity |
| **Self-service portal** | **بوابة الخدمة الذاتية** | Customer-facing interface for autonomous management | Standard technical term |

### Technical Security Terms

| Term (EN) | Term (AR) | Definition | Usage Notes |
|-----------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| **Zero Trust Architecture** | **بنية انعدام الثقة الأمنية** | Security model requiring verification for every access | Capitalize in English |
| **SIEM** | **نظام إدارة معلومات وأحداث الأمن السيبراني** | Security Information and Event Management | Keep acronym in English, spell out in Arabic |
| **XDR** | **الكشف والاستجابة الموسّعة** | Extended Detection and Response | Keep acronym in English |
| **Threat intelligence** | **المعلومات الاستخباراتية عن التهديدات** | Curated information about active threats | Professional security term |
| **Penetration testing** | **اختبار الاختراق** | Authorized simulated cyberattack | Standard Arabic term |
| **Vulnerability assessment** | **تقييم الثغرات الأمنية** | Systematic identification of security weaknesses | Common Arabic translation |
| **Firewall** | **جدار الحماية** | Network security system | Established Arabic term |
| **Encryption** | **التشفير** | Data protection through encoding | Standard term |

### Infrastructure & Cloud Terms

| Term (EN) | Term (AR) | Definition | Usage Notes |
|-----------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| **Hybrid cloud** | **السحابة المختلطة** | IT architecture combining private, public, on-premises | Standard translation |
| **Multi-cloud** | **متعدد السحابات** | Strategy using services from multiple cloud providers | Descriptive Arabic |
| **Disaster recovery** | **استعادة البيانات بعد الكوارث** | Strategies for restoring IT operations after failure | Formal Arabic term |
| **High availability** | **التوافر العالي** | System design ensuring maximum uptime | Technical term |
| **Backup** | **النسخ الاحتياطي** | Data protection through copying | Common term |
| **Migration** | **النقل** or **الترحيل** | Moving data/applications between systems | Both acceptable |
| **Data center** | **مركز البيانات** | Facility housing computer systems | Standard term |
| **Cloud computing** | **الحوسبة السحابية** | On-demand computing resources | Established term |

### Business & Service Terms

| Term (EN) | Term (AR) | Definition | Usage Notes |
|-----------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| **Mission-critical** | **حيوية للأعمال** or **بالغة الأهمية** | Systems essential to business operations | Use contextually |
| **ROI (Return on Investment)** | **العائد على الاستثمار** | Financial benefit relative to cost | Keep acronym in English |
| **SLA (Service Level Agreement)** | **اتفاقية مستوى الخدمة** | Formal commitment regarding service quality | Keep acronym in English |
| **Compliance** | **الامتثال** or **الالتزام** | Adherence to regulatory requirements | Both acceptable |
| **Uptime** | **وقت التشغيل** | Period when system is operational | Technical term |
| **Support** | **الدعم الفني** | Customer assistance | Add "technical" in Arabic |
| **Pricing** | **التسعير** or **الأسعار** | Cost structure | Both acceptable contextually |
| **Transparent** | **شفاف** or **واضح** | Open and clear | Core brand value |

## 8. Arabic Considerations

### Cultural Adaptations

**Formality Level:**
- **Business Context (Formal):** Use formal Arabic (الفصحى المبسطة) for main content, official communications, contracts
- **Support Context (Semi-formal):** Use accessible modern standard Arabic for support, FAQs, guides
- **Marketing Context (Engaging):** Balance professionalism with approachability—formal but not distant

**Tone Preferences:**
- Egyptian businesses expect professionalism and respect
- Avoid overly casual language in CTAs
- Use first person plural "نحن" (we) to build partnership feeling
- Address users with respectful "أنت/أنتم" forms

---

### Arabic-Specific Phrases & Idioms

| Context | Arabic Phrase | Literal Translation | When to Use |
|---------|--------------|---------------------|-------------|
| **Trust** | نحن معك في كل خطوة | We're with you every step | Partnership messaging |
| **Confidence** | بثقة تامة | With complete confidence | Security, guarantees |
| **Excellence** | معايير عالمية | Global standards | Quality messaging |
| **Partnership** | شريكك الموثوق | Your trusted partner | About section |
| **Commitment** | نلتزم بوعودنا | We honor our promises | Guarantee messaging |
| **Innovation** | نواكب التطور | We keep pace with development | Technology focus |
| **Support** | دعم لا ينقطع | Uninterrupted support | 24/7 messaging |

---

### Text Length Considerations

**Arabic text is typically 25-30% longer than English:**

**Design Implications:**
- **Buttons:** Allow 40% more space for Arabic labels
- **Headlines:** Arabic headlines may wrap to 2 lines where English is 1 line
- **Navigation:** Mobile menu items may need abbreviated forms
- **Forms:** Field labels in Arabic need more horizontal space

**Character Count Guidelines:**

| Element | EN Target | AR Target | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Page Title (H1) | 5-12 words | 4-10 words | Keep concise |
| Button CTA | 2-4 words | 2-3 words | Short imperative |
| Navigation Label | 1-2 words | 1-2 words | Single concept |
| Card Description | 20-30 words | 18-26 words | Shorter for space |
| Hero Subheadline | 15-25 words | 13-22 words | Adjust for length |

---

### RTL Layout Considerations

**Visual Direction:**
- All Arabic pages use RTL (right-to-left) layout
- Icons and arrows should flip horizontally
- Reading flow: Right → Left
- Navigation order: Right to left
- Form labels: Right-aligned

**Elements That Don't Flip:**
- English brand names (Roaya IT, WorldPosta, CloudEdge, Posta)
- Email addresses
- Phone numbers
- URLs
- Brand logos

**Number Formatting:**
- Use Arabic-Indic numerals (١٢٣) OR Western numerals (123) consistently
- **Recommendation:** Use Western numerals (123) for technical metrics for clarity
- Example: 99.9% stays as 99.9% (not ٩٩.٩٪)

---

### Translation Principles

**1. Meaning Over Literal Translation**
- "Zero Trust Architecture" → "بنية انعدام الثقة الأمنية" (emphasizes security aspect)
- "White-label" → "قابل للتخصيص بعلامتك التجارية" (explains the concept, not literal)

**2. Keep Technical Terms Where Appropriate**
- Email, WiFi, Cloud, VPN stay in English in Arabic text
- SAP, SOC, SIEM kept as acronyms with Arabic explanation on first mention

**3. Localize Business Concepts**
- "ROI" → "العائد على الاستثمار" (spell out in Arabic for clarity)
- "SLA" → "اتفاقية مستوى الخدمة" (familiar business term)

**4. Cultural Context**
- Emphasize "trust" (الثقة) and "security" (الأمان) more prominently in Arabic
- Use "guarantee" (نضمن) and "commitment" (التزام) frequently
- Reference "Egyptian" identity prominently (مصري)

---

### Common Arabic Mistakes to Avoid

**Wrong:**
- Literal word-for-word translation
- Using Egyptian colloquial dialect in formal business copy
- Inconsistent terminology (calling same thing different names)
- Mixing Western and Arabic-Indic numerals
- Google Translate without human review

**Right:**
- Professional, accessible modern standard Arabic
- Consistent use of established technical terms
- Cultural adaptation of messaging
- Professional translation + native speaker review
- Context-appropriate formality level

## 9. Voice & Tone Guidelines

### Brand Voice (EN & AR)

**Roaya IT brand voice is consistently:**

1. **Professional Authority** (احترافية وموثوقية)
   - EN: Expert without being condescending
   - AR: Use formal structures but accessible vocabulary

2. **Transparent & Direct** (شفافية ومباشرة)
   - EN: Clear, specific, no corporate jargon
   - AR: Plain language, avoid overly flowery phrases

3. **Trustworthy** (جديرة بالثقة)
   - EN: Back claims with evidence
   - AR: Emphasize commitment (التزام) and trust (ثقة)

4. **Partnership-Oriented** (موجهة نحو الشراكة)
   - EN: Use "we" and "you," build relationship
   - AR: "نحن معك" (we're with you) messaging

5. **Results-Focused** (مركزة على النتائج)
   - EN: Business outcomes, measurable ROI
   - AR: Concrete benefits, quantifiable results

### Words to Use (Power Vocabulary)

**English:**
- **Trust builders:** Guaranteed, proven, certified, trusted, transparent
- **Action verbs:** Transform, secure, optimize, migrate, automate
- **Differentiators:** First, only, exclusive, comprehensive, unified
- **Outcomes:** ROI, savings, uptime, efficiency, security

**Arabic:**
- **Trust:** مضمون (guaranteed), مثبت (proven), معتمد (certified), موثوق (trusted), شفاف (transparent)
- **Action:** حوّل (transform), أمّن (secure), حسّن (optimize), انقل (migrate), أتمت (automate)
- **Differentiators:** الأول (first), الوحيد (only), الحصري (exclusive), الشامل (comprehensive)
- **Outcomes:** عائد الاستثمار (ROI), توفيرات (savings), وقت التشغيل (uptime), الكفاءة (efficiency)

### Words to Avoid

**English:**
- Vague promises: "best," "fastest" (without proof)
- Buzzwords: synergy, paradigm shift, game-changer
- Passive voice: "can be done"
- Filler: very, really, quite, just

**Arabic:**
- Overly complex classical Arabic (فصحى قديمة)
- Colloquial Egyptian dialect in formal copy
- Excessive adjectives without substance
- Direct translations of English idioms that don't work in Arabic

## Content Patterns (Reusable Templates)

### Error Message Pattern

**EN:** `[Action] failed. [Reason]. [What to do next].`
Example: "Submission failed. Connection timed out. Please try again or contact support."

**AR:** `فشل [الإجراء]. [السبب]. [ما يجب فعله].`
Example: "فشل الإرسال. انتهت مهلة الاتصال. يرجى المحاولة مرة أخرى أو الاتصال بالدعم."

---

### Success Message Pattern

**EN:** `[Item/Action] [completed action]. [Next step].`
Example: "Request submitted. We'll contact you within 24 hours."

**AR:** `تم [الإجراء]. [الخطوة التالية].`
Example: "تم إرسال الطلب. سنتواصل معك خلال 24 ساعة."

---

### Empty State Pattern

**EN:** `[Current state]. [Invitation to action].`
Example: "No results found. Try different keywords or browse our solutions."

**AR:** `[الحالة الحالية]. [دعوة للإجراء].`
Example: "لم نعثر على نتائج. جرب كلمات مفتاحية مختلفة أو تصفح حلولنا."

---

### Confirmation Dialog Pattern

**EN:** `Are you sure you want to [action]? [Consequence].`
Example: "Are you sure you want to delete this? This action cannot be undone."

**AR:** `هل أنت متأكد من [الإجراء]؟ [النتيجة].`
Example: "هل أنت متأكد من الحذف؟ لا يمكن التراجع عن هذا الإجراء."
