### Challenge 1: Production Downtime Costs Thousands per Hour

**Headline:** Every minute of IT downtime stops production lines and costs revenue

**Copy:**

Your IT infrastructure must deliver 24/7 availability—production cannot wait for system recovery.

### Challenge 2: SAP/ERP Systems Are Single Points of Failure

**Headline:** SAP downtime cascades across the entire manufacturing operation

**Copy:**
Modern manufacturing depends entirely on ERP systems—SAP ECC, S/4HANA, Oracle EBS—for production scheduling, inventory management, quality control, and supply chain coordination. When your ERP goes offline, you cannot release production orders, verify material availability, or confirm customer shipments. Managing SAP infrastructure requires specialized expertise that most manufacturing IT teams lack.

### Challenge 3: Supply Chain Digitization Requires Real-Time Integration

**Headline:** Supply chain partners demand real-time data access and system integration

**Copy:**
Just-in-time manufacturing requires real-time visibility into supplier shipments, inventory levels, and production schedules. Your systems must integrate seamlessly with supplier portals, logistics platforms, and customer ordering systems. Legacy infrastructure struggles to support the API integrations, data synchronization, and uptime requirements that modern supply chains demand.

### Challenge 4: OT/IT Convergence Creates Security Vulnerabilities

**Headline:** Connected production equipment expands attack surface for cyber threats

**Copy:**
Industry 4.0 connects production machinery, sensors, and control systems to enterprise IT networks—creating pathways for cyber attacks to reach manufacturing operations. A ransomware attack targeting IT systems can spread to operational technology, shutting down production lines. Traditional IT security tools cannot protect industrial control systems without disrupting manufacturing operations.

### Challenge 5: ISO Standards Compliance Demands Continuous Monitoring

**Headline:** ISO 27001 and ISO 9001 certifications require documented IT controls
