### Challenge 1: Patient Data Breach Risk

**Headline:** Patient data breaches damage reputation and trigger severe penalties

**Copy:**
Healthcare organizations hold highly sensitive patient information—medical records, payment data, personal identifiers. A single breach exposes thousands of patients and can result in regulatory fines, legal liability, and irreversible reputation damage. Egyptian healthcare providers face increasing cyber threats targeting patient databases.

### Challenge 2: EHR System Downtime

**Headline:** Electronic health record downtime disrupts patient care

**Copy:**
When EHR systems go offline, clinical workflows stop. Doctors cannot access patient histories, pharmacists cannot verify prescriptions, and administrative staff cannot process admissions. Every minute of downtime delays treatment and frustrates staff. Healthcare organizations require 99.9% uptime for mission-critical systems.

### Challenge 3: Complex Compliance Requirements

**Headline:** Meeting HIPAA and Egyptian healthcare data protection standards requires expertise

**Copy:**
Healthcare compliance involves technical controls, administrative policies, and continuous monitoring. Organizations must protect patient data at rest and in transit, restrict access based on roles, maintain audit logs, and demonstrate compliance during inspections. Many IT teams lack specialized healthcare security expertise.

### Challenge 4: Legacy System Integration

**Headline:** Medical devices and legacy systems create security vulnerabilities

**Copy:**
Hospitals run a complex mix of modern EHR platforms, older laboratory systems, and networked medical devices—many with outdated operating systems or no security updates. These legacy systems create entry points for attackers. Securing this heterogeneous environment without disrupting clinical operations requires specialized knowledge.

### Challenge 5: Telemedicine Infrastructure Demands

**Headline:** Telemedicine growth demands secure, scalable, high-performance infrastructure

**Copy:**
Post-COVID telemedicine adoption has accelerated dramatically across Egypt. Video consultations, remote patient monitoring, and digital prescription systems require secure hosting, reliable bandwidth, and seamless integration with existing EHR platforms. Infrastructure must support growing patient volumes without performance degradation.
